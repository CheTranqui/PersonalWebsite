Enhancing Digital Experiences

I'm Chaz, known across the web as CheTranqui1. I began this journey into Software Development informally in 2017 with the goal of improving the player experience in Heroes of the Storm. Despite the challenge, I enjoyed the process immensely. I've since discovered that my primary driver is improving user experience through thoughtful feature design and creating meaningful interactions. Providing such an experience is always rewarding.

What Inspired Me To Become A Developer

This was my first ever project back in 2017. I wanted to learn how to draft better and decided to create a minigame that would let me draft against professional teams and critique my choices. Most people start off with 'Hello World', I started off with C#, Unity, and how to import data from a JSON file. It was a rough couple of months, but I'm proud of the not-quite-finished result.

GitHub Video Download

In 2018 I revisited a MUD that I enjoyed during my teenage years called Dragonrealms. To make several travel quicker and simpler I made the script that I am most proud of: "Go Anywhere". It uses pivot points connected via major corridors, and moves the character using a destination variable (%D) where %Dto%D results in arrival. It has around 300 starting and ending locations and starts with a simple ".go place". It's also highly communicative with an in-game menu and directions upon arrival.

"Go" Script More Scripts

In 2018 I was deeply engaged in a newer game called Empires & Puzzles. Since it didn't have much of a wiki (90 pages, all basic hero cards), I wanted to improve it. Over the next 4 months I created approximately 350 pages. I organized the content into small logical pieces and built the backbone of what is available today. When I started it was receiving less than 1000 views per month. In January of 2021 it had over 600,000 views and my Hero Information page received over 14,000 alone.

1: "¡Che, tranqui!" is a common phrase heard on the streets of Argentina on a daily basis. "Che" is a way to call out to another person in the same way as we use "Dude" or "Bro" here in the United States. "Tranqui" is a command, short for "tranquilo" meaning relax or chill. I've combined the two into an idiomatic expression to imply my goal to be a chill, relaxed guy.
